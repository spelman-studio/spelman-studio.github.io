Food Truck Business

Branding • Business Strategy • Finance

This project outlines a full business plan for Bierwagen, a mobile German food truck serving authentic festival-style cuisine. The plan includes financial modeling, market research, operations planning, and a marketing strategy focused on festivals and large outdoor events.

Project Overview

Bierwagen specializes in traditional German foods like bratwurst, pretzels, and potato pancakes served with dips such as applesauce, beer cheese, sauerkraut, and spicy mustard. The menu also includes premium German beers, with non-alcoholic options available for families.

The primary audience is adults attending Oktoberfest-style events who are interested in high-quality food and beer pairings. Families are a secondary audience, with simple, kid-friendly menu options.

The truck operates at outdoor festivals from Thursday through Sunday during warmer months. A typical order includes a main item, a dip, and a drink (purchased by about 75% of customers). Pricing is positioned as premium, with strong margins on house-made items.

Project Details

  • Role: Business Strategist & Financial Modeler
  • Tools: Financial Modeling (Excel), Market Research, Capacity Analysis
  • Category: Business Planning & Strategy
  • Year: 2025

Design Process

Research

Market research focused on festival attendees and families at outdoor events. Operational capacity allows for up to 300 bratwursts per hour, though expected demand ranges from 30-60 customers per hour due to early brand awareness. Projections assume steady growth from 50 to 60 customers per hour, with most customers purchasing drinks.

Concept Development

Financial models were built around a typical order (main + dip + drink). Costs were calculated by ingredient and adjusted with category-based markups. Staffing includes four team members handling cooking, orders, and service. The model accounts for rising costs, including a 10% increase in food costs and a 15% annual increase in overall expenses. Beer sales are emphasized to improve margins.

Financial Analysis

The business performs strongly in Year 1, generating $817K in revenue with a 41% gross margin. However, margins decline to 18% by Year 5 as costs grow faster than revenue. Marketing accounts for 20% of revenue in Year 1, with a low customer acquisition cost that is quickly recovered per order. The business is not limited by production capacity.

Final Outcome

The plan demonstrates that Bierwagen is a viable business with strong initial performance. However, long-term success depends on improving margins through menu adjustments and increased focus on high-profit items like beverages.

Marketing efforts, including a website, social media ads, and design tools, support efficient customer acquisition. Startup costs are estimated at $38.5K, including the truck and necessary equipment.
